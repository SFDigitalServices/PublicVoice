[Office of the CIO] Message

Miguel A. Gamiño, Jr.
City CIO of San Francisco and Executive Director of the Department of Technology

The Office of the CIO is uniquely positioned to provide Citywide technology vision and leadership while also serving as Director of the Department of Technology.

In this dual role, the OCIO advises the Mayor, Board of Supervisors and City departments regarding opportunities to leverage technology to improve government services; formulate the vision for Citywide coordination, embrace best practices and instigate enhanced technology; establish unified standards throughout the Citywide organization; and play a leadership role in the development and implementation of the Information and Communication Technology (ICT) plan for the City and County of San Francisco.

San Francisco  Technology Leadership

How the OCIO interacts with the City

The City Chief Information Officer is the Executive Director of the San Francisco Department of Technology, the enterprise information and technology services organization that delivers mission critical technology infrastructure and services.

The Department’s vision is to improve government services, the community, and the lives of the City and County of San Francisco customers through proactive leadership in the use of technology and information solutions.

San Francisco Wins A Best of the Web Award

September 01, 2016

San Francisco, CA —Today, The Center for Digital Government recognizes the City and County of San Francisco’s Department of Technology with a Best of the Web Award. The department earned the award for its approach to migrating 115 websites in the sfgov.org collection of sites; integrating open-source solutions, to better serve its residents, businesses and visitors.

San Francisco Celebrates World Wi-Fi Day

August 30, 2016

San Francisco, CA —Today, the Department of Technology announced 16 new access points added to the #SFWiFi network. The upgrades are intended to increase the network’s capacity to support greater density during large-scale events along Market Street, allowing more users to connect to the network simultaneously.
