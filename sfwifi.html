#SFWiFi
Pervasive, High-Speed Internet Access for San Francisco

San Francisco provides free wireless internet access, called #SFWiFi, for use by the general public, managed by the Department of Technology.

The network is available in 33 parks and public spaces, all public libraries, over three miles of the Market Street corridor and seven San Francisco Municipal Transportation Authority (SFMTA) stations: Embarcadero, Montgomery, Powell, Civic Center, Van Ness, Church and Castro. The network delivers service typically far-exceeding Federal Communications Commission defined broadband speeds of 25Mbps.

The San Francisco Department of Technology also manages the CCSF WiFi network, which is available in City buildings for employees.

The San Francisco Department of Technology provides the following services for Internet connectivity:

  • site surveys to ensure service coverage
  • facility wiring
  • configuration and installation of the wireless infrastructure
  • connections to ensure Internet access
  • ongoing support of network infrastructure and wireless access points

FAQs

How do I access free, public #SFWiFi?
To connect to #SFWiFi: i. Select the WiFi network “#SFWiFi” when selecting a network on your device ii. Launch a web browser
How do I report trouble accessing #SFWiFi?
If you have issues connecting in available areas, tweet your problem and location and include the hashtag #SFWiFi. Or call 311.
Are there plans to expand #SFWiFi?
We are developing a strategic plan to further connectivity expansion for the public benefit in various locations across the City. We welcome the opportunity to work collaboratively with organizations and members of the public in ensuring that high speed internet connectivity is accessible for all our residents.
How fast is the WiFi?
The network delivers service typically far-exceeding Federal Communications Commission defined broadband speeds of 25Mbps.
Is my information safe while using the network?
#SFWiFi is open to anyone and caution should be taken to protect your private and financial information.
Is #SFWiFi use monitored?
Usage is not monitored, only system statistics are being gathered: • WiFi system health and stability • Performance • Connection speeds
