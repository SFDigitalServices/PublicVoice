San Francisco Wins A Best of the Web Award

San Francisco, CA —Today, The Center for Digital Government recognizes the City and County of San Francisco’s Department of Technology with a Best of the Web Award. The department earned the award for its approach to migrating 115 websites in the sfgov.org collection of sites; integrating open-source solutions, to better serve its residents, businesses and visitors.

“We’re committed to modernizing government and improving service delivery to the public,” said Miguel Gamiño, City and County Chief Information Officer and Executive Director of the Department of Technology. “These awards encourage us to double down on our promise to leverage technology to be more responsive to our constituents.”

The change deployed a more flexible, cost efficient system that improves access across a variety of devices than the proprietary solution that previously hosted the sites. Visitors now experience substantially improved site response and increased site reliability. Additionally, the sites are now mobile responsive, an important factor in user experience as smartphone adoption continues to increase.

In its report, U.S. Smartphone Use in 2015, Pew Research found that 64% of American adults now own a smartphone, up from 35% in the spring of 2011. 15% of Americans own a smartphone but say they have a limited number of ways to get online other than their cell phone. Overall, the move provides a better experience for residents and visitors accessing city information on their phones.

More than 100 city services are available via sfgov.org, which serves as a gateway to the 115 other sites on the domain. The portal is a mechanism for delivering a range of services including delivering public records documents and collecting payments online. In 2015, San Francisco collected more than $1 billion dollars via online payments. Delivering a range of services online increases transparency throughout local government.

The Center for Digital Government awards San Francisco’s Department of Technology Digital Government Achievement Awards in the County Portal and Government-to-citizen local government categories

The redesign leverages the Agile methodology and open-source technologies—Drupal and a fully open-source LAMP Stack—to deliver the website quickly and effectively, an uncommon practice in the government sector. The use of free and open-source software yields substantial savings by eliminating the fees and licensing associated with proprietary software.

Each year the Digital Government Achievement Awards recognize a broad range of digital accomplishments in government for outstanding agency and department websites and projects at the application and infrastructure level. 

 

The Center for Digital Government is a national research and advisory institute on information technology policies and best practices in state and local government. Through its diverse and dynamic programs and services, the Center provides public and private sector leaders with decision support, knowledge, and opportunities to help them effectively incorporate new technologies in the 21st century.

Kathleen Clark

Kathleen is the Communications Director for the San Francisco Department of Technology, managing public and media relations on behalf of the OCIO and the Department of Technology.

San Francisco Celebrates World Wi-Fi Day

August 30, 2016

San Francisco, CA —Today, the Department of Technology announced 16 new access points added to the #SFWiFi network. The upgrades are intended to increase the network’s capacity to support greater density during large-scale events along Market Street, allowing more users to connect to the network simultaneously.
