SF Cloud
Compute, Storage and Data Protection Services for CCSF

San Francisco offers compute, storage and data protection services under SF Cloud. Compute and storage services allow you to run your application and business processes, and store your business data. Data protection ensures you have data backups.

City assets at data centers together with public cloud services provide a hybrid service offering including both infrastructure-as-a-service (IaaS) and platform-as-a-service (PaaS).

Today SF Cloud offers City and County of San Francisco departments the following features:

  • a virtual machine environment
  • monitoring
  • security & compliance reporting
  • network services
  • disaster recovery
  • virtual assessments & migrations

FAQs

Q. What is your process for unplanned system interruption or outage?
Communications will be sent to the affected departments within 30 minutes of the outage, and will be followed by hourly updates as needed. A debrief communication will be sent within 24 hours of return to service regarding the cause of the outage, and if relevant, any steps to prevent a reoccurrence in the future. Service will be returned as quickly as possible.
What is a virtual machine and what value does it bring?
Virtual machine emulates a physical computer. The advantage of a virtual machine is that many virtual machines can run on one physical machine. Benefits include efficient use of physical hardware, less heat and therefore energy savings, quicker provisioning for customers, and a smaller data center footprint.
Does the City encourage the move to virtualization?
Yes. Under the Virtual First policy, IT managers across the City and County of San Francisco are required to explore virtualization options prior to submitting requests for new or replacement servers.
How do City departments and customers know if their physical servers are good candidates for virtualization?
Part of the service the SF Cloud team offers is assessing the customer environment to see if it can be migrated at all. If so, the team then moves ahead and provisions a virtual environment for the customer and trains the customer on how to manage it.
