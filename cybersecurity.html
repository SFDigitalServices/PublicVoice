Cybersecurity
Ensuring data confidentiality, integrity and availability

DT Security Services provides support to the Information Security (InfoSec) staff of all San Francisco City and County departments and agencies. As DT is the primary network and datacenter services provider for the City, the department is uniquely positioned to monitor and assess threats and provide information on outbreaks with Security Services.

Security Services aims to help CCSF maintain the CIA triad:

  • Protect the confidentiality of data
  • Preserve the integrity of data

Promote the availability of data for authorized use.

FAQs

How do I request or learn more about a cybersecurity service?
Call (415) 581-7100 or email: dt-securityservices@sfgov.org
What is Threat Assessment & Monitoring?
The Security Services team has a number of tools with which we can provide a strategic understanding and real-time monitoring of the information security threats to your organization and infrastructure.
What is eDiscovery?
Electronic discovery (eDiscovery) is the process of electronic data is sought, located, secured, and searched with the intent of using it as evidence in a civil or criminal legal case. eDiscovery can be performed on individual computers, applications with repositories e.g. Salesforece.com or Microsoft Exchange, or on network logs.
What is Security Awareness Training and How Do I Get Involved?
The Security Services team provides security education and awareness training to City employees to provide staff with current information and access to tools that help manage risk.
How Can My Department Prepare for a Disaster?
The Security Services team has best practices and tools to the backups and other resources necessary to provide continuity of operations in worst case scenarios.
